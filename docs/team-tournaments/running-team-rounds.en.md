# Running team rounds

Once the teams are set up and a [pairing system]({% link docs/team-tournaments/pairing-systems.en.md %})
is chosen, each round follows the same loop. Most of it mirrors an individual
event; this page covers what is specific to teams.

## Line-ups

A team's **line-up** for a round is the order in which its players occupy the
boards — board 1 is the top board.

- By default it is the first _N_ players of the **roster** (where _N_ is the
  number of boards per team), in roster order.
- You can **override** the line-up for a given round — for example to rest a
  player or field a reserve. Gaps are allowed.

## Check-in

Teams **check in as a unit** before they can be paired — checking in a team marks
it present for the round. You can do this from the admin screen or from a
dedicated check-in screen on another device.

## Pairings and the match

Generate the round's pairings as usual. A team pairing is a **match**: a set of
boards between two teams (or, under [Molter]({% link docs/team-tournaments/molter-tables.en.md %}),
individual boards across teams). Colours follow the system.

## Entering results

Enter each **board** result; the match result aggregates automatically into game
points — and match points on the systems that have them. As in an individual
event, players can enter their own board results from a screen, and the arbiter
confirms.

## Byes

- **Pairing-allocated bye (PAB)** — with an odd number of teams, **Team Swiss**
  gives one team a bye each round. Its score is configurable (see
  [Scoring]({% link docs/team-tournaments/scoring-tie-breaks.en.md %})).
- **Manual byes** — the arbiter can give a team a **half-point (HPB)**,
  **full-point (FPB)** or **zero-point (ZPB)** bye for a round, subject to the
  tournament's bye permissions.
